Padparadscha Sapphire

"A rare and exquisite variety"

Characteristics

  • Unique pink-orange color combination
  • Extremely rare variety of sapphire
  • High durability (9 on Mohs scale)
  • Natural color without treatment

History and Significance

The name "Padparadscha" comes from the Sinhalese word for lotus blossom, reflecting its unique pink-orange color. These rare gems are among the most valuable and sought-after sapphires in the world.

Mining and Origin

Sri Lanka is the primary source of natural Padparadscha sapphires. These gems are found in the alluvial deposits of the island's gem-bearing regions.

Quality Factors

Color

The ideal color is a delicate blend of pink and orange, reminiscent of a tropical sunset.

Rarity

Among the rarest of all sapphire varieties, making them highly valuable.

Care and Maintenance

  • Clean with mild soap and warm water
  • Avoid ultrasonic cleaners
  • Store separately from other gemstones
  • Protect from extreme temperature changes
